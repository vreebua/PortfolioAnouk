Data-driven Chatbot graduation project

In order to help young adults properly dispose of their plastic, I created a data-driven personal chatbot application for my master's graduation project.

×

Data-driven Chatbot graduation project

Time spent on it: 20 weeks, year 2 Master
Language of the project: English
How many people worked on it: 1

For my graduation project, I created a data-driven application that helps young people dispose of their plastic products properly. This was solved by a smart chatbot that provides information about the type of plastic and where it should be disposed of according to city regulations. The chatbot also asks personal questions about why the user cares about the environment and other related opinions. The user's answers help the chatbot to offer a personalized gamification strategy to help the user properly dispose of their plastic. This program uses six different reasons why people recycle, including attitude, subjective norms, behavioral intention, knowledge, moral norms and social pressure. Each category is linked to a research-based gamification strategy. If the chatbot detects more, the user can be subjected to multiple forms of gamification.

Using data and AI to create music

By applying data cleansing, Artificial Intelligence and the RNN-LSTM model, I was able to construct a new generated sheet of music

×

Using data and AI to create music

Time spent on it: 10 weeks, year 1 Master
Language of the project: English
How many people worked on it: 1

For the machine learning lesson, I intended to create music using Artificial Intelligence and the RNN-LSTM model. This project is separated into four steps: data collection, data cleaning, model construction, and model evaluation. I wanted to create jazz music, namely a jazz sheet. To begin, I acquired a significant number of jazz songs. In the second phase, data cleansing, I compared jazz songs based on parameters including tempo, number of measures, style, and density. I trimmed my jazz music dataset by only including jazz songs that matched in all of these categories, so that the model might have a higher success rate later on. This model is known as RNN-LSTM. This model can predict the next note in a melody. Reading comparable jazz songs allows it to guess what note will be played next, resulting in a new note at the end. By repeating this procedure, a whole new melody can be created, and therefore a song can be formed. I recommend reading this more clearly in my GitHub ipnyb files, as they cover the entire procedure in detail, including preprocessing, encoding, sequence creation, mapping, converting, training, layering, and producing.

Artificial Intelligence Movie Recommender

Some code recommends movies based on the user's most recent Twitter tweets using Artificial Intelligence and API's.

×

Artificial Intelligence Movie Recommender

Time spent on it: 10 weeks, year 3 Bachelor
Language of the project: Dutch
How many people worked on it: 1

My first exposure to Artificial Intelligence was through my project AI Movie Recommender. The assignment's primary goal was to learn as much as possible about this emerging technology, but with the end goal of creating a product. After learning about the technology and brainstorming ideas, the end result is a website (without visuals) that assists the user in selecting a Netflix movie or show based on their most recent Twitter posts. The AI was able to read Twitter posts and determine from the text what kind of emotion the user was feeling and what the subject of their post was about, with the help of Google Natural Language and a Twitter API. "I am excited about this wedding," for example, would tell the AI that "excited" is a positive word and "wedding" is a subject. Following that, the AI scans the Netflix database for movies or series, as well as the tags they provide, that can be related to the discovered emotions and words, in order to recommend them.

Internet of Things Doorway Effect Helper

×

Internet of Things Doorway Effect Helper

Time spent on it: 3 weeks, year 1 Master
Language of the project: English
How many people worked on it: 1

A three-week timeframe was set for this project, with the purpose of swiftly understanding a new developing technology and how it can solve a problem. My chosen technology was the Internet of Things (IoT), which I worked with in the Arduino Program using an Arduino board. The issue I chose is known as the front doorway effect. Internet of Things Mobile Malware Detecter

An experiment in which a mobile phone is linked to Internet of Things sensors to assist in determining whether or not malware is present on the phone.

×

Internet of Things Mobile Malware Detecter

Time spent on it: 3 weeks, year 1 Master
Language of the project: English
How many people worked on it: 1

This project was given a short time frame of three weeks. This assignment's goal was to learn how to quickly master a new upcoming technology. Following some brainstorming, the idea was born that malware could be detected on a mobile phone using the Internet of Things (IoT). If a mobile phone becomes infected with malware, it may exhibit a variety of symptoms, including high temperature. I thought I'd connect the two because IoT is known for its ability to identify certain sensors and immediately communicate that data. As a result, I used an application to set up a connection between an old mobile phone and my Arduino board. This connection was constantly reading and reporting the temperature of the phone. The phone had become infected with malware. If the Arduino board detects strange sparks of higher temperature inside the phone, it will alert the user using other IoT products that can communicate via sound and visual.

Make your own maze in Unity

An interactive maze created with a depth-first search algorithm.

×

Make your own maze in Unity

Time spent on it: 1 week
Language of the project: English
How many people worked on it: 1

In a free project I decided to learn more about the C# language and make a maze generator. I chose a depth-first algorithm. With this algorithm, you start at a random point and keep digging paths in one of 4 directions (up, right, down, left) until you can't go any further. If stuck, the maze moves back one step until it finds another open path. This keeps repeating until the maze is finished. In addition to the algorithm, it is also interactive with a menu screen. In this menu screen the user can choose and adjust the length and width of the maze and see how the maze is built. Also, the user can decide in the menu if they want to walk in it after the maze is built. If so, the camera is changed to a first person view and you can walk with the "wasd" keys and rotate with the mouse.
