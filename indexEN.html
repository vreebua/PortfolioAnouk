Data-driven Chatbot graduation project

In order to help young adults properly dispose of their plastic, I created a data-driven personal chatbot application for my master's graduation project.

×

Data-driven Chatbot graduation project

Time spent on it: 20 weeks, year 2 Master
Language of the project: English
How many people worked on it: 1

I had to create a data-driven application that solved an issue for my Masters graduation project. I chose to go the environmentally friendly approach and assist young individuals in correctly disposing of their plastic products, rather than littering or tossing them in the wrong container. This issue could be remedied with the help of a smart chatbot integrated into an application. The chatbot would assist the young adult in providing information about their plastic product, including what type of plastic it is and where it should be thrown according to city regulations. The chatbot would also ask the user personal questions about why they care about the environment and other related opinions on the subject. The answers provided by the user would assist the chatbot in better categorising the user, allowing it to offer a personalised gamification strategy that would help drive the user to continue properly disposing of their plastic. The categories are based on research, which state that there are six different reasons why people recycle, which are as follows: attitude, subjective norms, behavioural intent, knowledge, moral norms, and social pressure. If a user tells the chatbot that they conduct environmental acts because they have seen others do it, they will be given a gamification approach in that area. Each category can be coupled with a research-based gamification strategy. Attitude → points system, subjective norms → leaderbord, perceived behavioural control → badges, knowledge → narrative, moral norms → rewards, social pressure → teams. If the chatbot detects more, the user can be subjected to multiple forms of gamification. As a result, the program is constantly enhancing the user experience based on user data, thereby establishing it as a successful data-driven product. Furthermore, the application provides information about different types of plastic, how it works in their location, and opportunities to speed up the recycling process. More information on the research and overall project can be found in the papers below.

Using data and AI to create music

By applying data cleansing, Artificial Intelligence and the RNN-LSTM model, I was able to construct a new generated sheet of music

×

Using data and AI to create music

Time spent on it: 10 weeks, year 1 Master
Language of the project: English
How many people worked on it: 1

For the machine learning lesson, I intended to create music using Artificial Intelligence and the RNN-LSTM model. This project is separated into four steps: data collection, data cleaning, model construction, and model evaluation. I wanted to create jazz music, namely a jazz sheet. To begin, I acquired a significant number of jazz songs. In the second phase, data cleansing, I compared jazz songs based on parameters including tempo, number of measures, style, and density. I trimmed my jazz music dataset by only including jazz songs that matched in all of these categories, so that the model might have a higher success rate later on. This model is known as RNN-LSTM. This model can predict the next note in a melody. Reading comparable jazz songs allows it to guess what note will be played next, resulting in a new note at the end. By repeating this procedure, a whole new melody can be created, and therefore a song can be formed. I recommend reading this more clearly in my GitHub ipnyb files, as they cover the entire procedure in detail, including preprocessing, encoding, sequence creation, mapping, converting, training, layering, and producing.

Artificial Intelligence Movie Recommender

Time spent on it: 10 weeks, year 3 Bachelor
Language of the project: Dutch
How many people worked on it: 1

My first exposure to Artificial Intelligence was through my project AI Movie Recommender. The assignment's primary goal was to learn as much as possible about this emerging technology, but with the end goal of creating a product. After learning about the technology and brainstorming ideas, the end result is a website (without visuals) that assists the user in selecting a Netflix movie or show based on their most recent Twitter posts. Internet of Things Mobile Malware Detecter

An experiment in which a mobile phone is linked to Internet of Things sensors to assist in determining whether or not malware is present on the phone.

×

Internet of Things Mobile Malware Detecter

Time spent on it: 3 weeks, year 1 Master
Language of the project: English
How many people worked on it: 1

This project was given a short time frame of three weeks. This assignment's goal was to learn how to quickly master a new upcoming technology. Following some brainstorming, the idea was born that malware could be detected on a mobile phone using the Internet of Things (IoT). If a mobile phone becomes infected with malware, it may exhibit a variety of symptoms, including high temperature. I thought I'd connect the two because IoT is known for its ability to identify certain sensors and immediately communicate that data. As a result, I used an application to set up a connection between an old mobile phone and my Arduino board. This connection was constantly reading and reporting the temperature of the phone. The phone had become infected with malware. If the Arduino board detects strange sparks of higher temperature inside the phone, it will alert the user using other IoT products that can communicate via sound and visual.

DataSee, an Augmented Reality application

×

DataSee, an Augmented Reality application

Time spent on it: 10 weeks, year 1 Master
Language of the project: English
How many people worked on it: 1

DataSee is an Augmented Reality (AR) application that helps enhance young adults' awareness about how much personal data they spend on their apps and how harmful that might be. It is able to show their data back in a fun, engaging, and interactive way by collecting their screen time data on a daily basis. After permitting the program to collect screen time data, the user will see their own application use reflected back to them in the form of data blobs, which can alter from day to day. Furthermore, each application data blob is a different colour, suggesting to the user whether or not this application is secure to disclose personal data on, or if it is easy subject to hacker assaults. The program expands on this theme by include components such as what would happen to their data if a hacker attacked the application, questions and quizzes about online safety, and more in-depth information about each application and how to be more secure with their data.

The 3D story driven game Schildknaap

I and my team produced a 3D story game during my minor in game design, and my role in this project was game designer with some programming.

×

The 3D story driven game Schildknaap

Time spent on it: Around 10 weeks, game minor, year 3 Bachelor
Language of the project: Dutch
How many people worked on it: 5

This game was created in a team of five during the minor Applied Game Design. Everyone on our team had a specific role, with mine being the lead game designer and side programmer. The game's intended audience is pupils aged twelve and up who are visiting a history museum. The goal of this game is to collect as much as you can in a limited amount of time to progress a story. In level 1, for example, you visit a castle as a 3D knight and must acquire items such as swords, horses, rope and wheels. That is, if you can find all of them. When the timer runs out, the user can utilise the collected things to build a tale. The more items you collect, the more likely it is that you will be able to create a compelling story. For example, "the knight must combat a bear; what weapon do they use?" If a sword was collected, it might be utilised to change the course of the story. There are three levels in total, and the plot progresses with each level. The narrative of the story is set in medieval times, allowing the target audience to learn about history in a fascinating way. You triumph when you successfully complete the story. If you collect too few objects, critical steps in the game may be skipped or answered wrong, preventing you from reaching the finish and forcing you to restart.
